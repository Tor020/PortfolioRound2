About

At time of writing in 2017, I'm a 26 year old self taught Web developer whose been using the Internet since the late 90s. I've always thought the Internet was pretty awesome, and figuring out how things work has always been something I've enjoyed doing.

I find Web development to be really engaging and fun for me personally because it feels like there's great practical application to all the new things I learn. For instance, I loved learning how to make the Pong demo because when I started with Web development, I had no idea that making games via HTML5 and Javascript was even possible. It was really exciting for me to see that I could utilize the canvas and Javascript to make something like that. It was just really cool for me.

As far as short term goals, I'm really dedicated to understanding Javascript and working to be able to utilize it to its full potential. This includes things like learning the nuances of modern Javascript frameworks. I have long term aspirations of becoming a fullstack developer.

A value i find important is trying your best when doing things. In a sense, I look at trying my best as a goal for anything. I think that having that initial goal in your mind helps give you a frame of reference for what you are trying to do with all things. This type of thinking is important because of the real world implications it would have. This is one of those things that probably sounds good in theory but if you stop to consider the idea you might see a problem, which is we are all humans not machines, perfection is unattainable and people have lives. Sometimes people have problems at home, sometimes people are distracted from the task at hand. Shit, sometimes it's just more fun to not care and do the task in some other way. The way i feel about this is that trying your best is just an extremely good framework to deal with anything. You don't go into a task and size up the situation and think about deadlines etc. What you always want to do is be able to walk away from a situation knowing that you did everything you could given what you knew and understood at the time. Transparency is vital. Try to imagine what the world would be like if everyone tried their best all the time. Depending on your outlook on the world mileage may vary. For me personally though, I think people are for the most part good. I think that if everyone tried their best at things they did that the world would be a better place for everyone. This is the type of perspective I strive to live with.

The world is very complicated. It's hard to boil down any one person's ideology to a few words to represent themselves, but I've thought about this type of thing quite a lot.

Being practical, Trying my best
